## Specific Research Aims

### Aim 1: Identify Prebiotic Metabolites
- Comparative metabolomics of healthy vs. infected tomato root exudates
- Network analysis to link metabolites with suppressive microbial taxa
- Structural elucidation of key prebiotic compounds

### Aim 2: Validate Suppressive Efficacy  
- Modified Leonard Jar system validation
- Temporal microbiome succession analysis
- Disease severity index monitoring

### Aim 3: Develop Scalable Formulations
- Seed coating and granular amendment development
- Shelf-life stability testing
- Field trial protocol design

## Innovation and Impact
- First application of multi-trophic prebiotic approach to bacterial wilt
- Sustainable alternative to chemical pesticides
- Potential for broad-spectrum disease management
